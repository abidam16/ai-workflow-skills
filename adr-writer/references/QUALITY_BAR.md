# ADR Quality Bar

A good ADR is:

- **Single-decision**: it records one important decision only.
- **Architecturally meaningful**: the decision has lasting impact or broad consequences.
- **Bounded**: the decision boundary is explicit.
- **Contextualized**: the problem and pressure that required the decision are clear.
- **Comparative**: serious alternatives are listed.
- **Justified**: the chosen option is explained against decision drivers.
- **Honest about trade-offs**: negative consequences are recorded, not hidden.
- **Connected**: downstream impact on PRD, roadmap, plans, implementation, or review is stated.
- **Lightweight**: concise enough to read quickly.

An ADR is weak if it:
- bundles unrelated decisions
- states a choice without real options
- hides trade-offs
- duplicates PRD or roadmap content
- rewrites prior decisions instead of superseding them with a new ADR
