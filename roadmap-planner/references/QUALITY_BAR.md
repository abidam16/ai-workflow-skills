# Roadmap Quality Bar

A roadmap is good if it:
- preserves approved product or technical intent
- organizes delivery around meaningful outcomes
- explains why phases are ordered the way they are
- captures dependencies, risks, and deferrals clearly
- gives planning enough context without collapsing into task lists
- distinguishes what is now, next, later, and explicitly not included

A roadmap is weak if it:
- reads like a backlog
- reads like a vague strategy note
- lacks exit criteria
- mixes multiple unrelated initiatives without clear structure
- forces every new initiative into an old maturity ladder that no longer fits
